Common questions answered

Everything you're
probably wondering.

How does the deposit work at auction?

Your deposit is split into two payments. An initial $10,000 is due on the day of purchase, with the balance of 5% of the purchase price due by 5pm the following business day.

Both payments go to the Place Camp Hill Sales Trust Account at Commonwealth Bank. Use an abbreviated version of the street address as your reference — e.g. 25 Bloggs Street = BLOG25. Once transferred, send your remittance to hicksteam@eplace.com.au.

Do I need a solicitor or can I use a conveyancer?

Either is perfectly fine. A licensed conveyancer handles the legal transfer of property and is often the most cost-effective choice. A solicitor offers broader legal advice if your situation is more complex.

We've listed two firms we trust further down this page, or feel free to choose any Queensland-licensed practitioner of your choice.

When can I do a pre-settlement inspection?

Your pre-settlement inspection will typically take place on the day of settlement or the day before. This is your opportunity to walk through the property and confirm it's in the same condition as when you purchased it and that all agreed inclusions are present.

Contact us to arrange a time — we'll coordinate access with the vendor.

When do I get the keys?

Keys are handed over on settlement day once funds have been confirmed as received. Your solicitor will notify you when settlement is complete — usually by midday. We'll be in touch as soon as we hear to arrange key collection or delivery.

What happens if something goes wrong before settlement?

Call us first. As an unconditional contract, it's important everything is in place on time. If you have any concerns about your funds, solicitor, or anything else, please reach out early — we're here to help you through to the finish line.
